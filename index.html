The Rise of the Taliban and the Crisis that Followed

In the year since American troops withdrew from Afghanistan, the Taliban's rapid takeover has caused a decades-long humanitarian crisis to deteriorate to new depths. While the world's attention has largely been elsewhere, millions of Afghans have seen their living conditions, freedoms, and life prospects diminish under Taliban rule.

This project seeks to:

(1) Tell the story of the Taliban takeover
(2) Describe the consequences for the people of Afghanistan

Taliban Takeover

In Early 2020 The United States and the Taliban signed an agreement aimed at ending the eighteen-year war in Afghanistan.


The biggest challenge was to achieve a peace agreement between the Taliban and the Afghan government. Continued Taliban violence, a weak Afghan government, and a gradual waning support by the west would lead to a total take over by the Taliban and the collapse of the Afghan government.

Fatalities during the Taliban offense

The immediate consequence of the Taliban’s advances is the resulting casualties suffered throughout the country. The last few months leading to the fall of Kabul were extremely violent, resulting a death toll in the many tens of thousands.

Our second visual explores chronologically and spatially the death resulting from the Taliban’s advances. Our data comes from The Armed Conflict Location & Event Data Project (ACLED) which collects real-time data on the locations, dates, actors, fatalities, and types of all reported political violence and protest events around the world.



Scroll to the next page to view a map of the fatalities suffered during the Taliban take over.

Recorded Fatalities by District from May to August 2021

Reported By ACLEAD

From April to August 2021 ACLEAD reported over 33 thousand deaths, 3 times larger than the same time period in 2020.


The largest amount of deaths occurred in the Lashkar Gah district in Kandahar, an economically important district and province.


Most of the conflicts occurred in bordering districts and provinces and around the capital Kabul, part of the Taliban’s military strategy was to target districts with major border crossings to bordering neighboring countries, effectively chocking off major trade routes and roads.

The Crisis' Human Cost

Events in Afghanistan over the past 20 years have been devastating for millions of Afghans who've experienced violence, famine, repression, and economic hardship. The Taliban takeover has only worsened their suffering.

Gallup's yearly survey of Afghan residents reveals a staggering level of hardship and hopelessness, the extent of which may surprise you.

How the World is Failing the Afghan People


After decades of war and famine, 3 out of 4 Afgans (over 29 million) are in need of humanitarian assistance. The Taliban takeover has only worsened the situation. Humanitarian funding needs have skyrocketed to $4.4B - 3x higher than in 2021 and 10x higher than 2015. Only 66% of funding needs are currently being met. Aid mostly plateaued after 2021, leaving humanitarian causes underfunding by $1.5B.
